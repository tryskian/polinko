About

I work across AI evaluation, human-AI interaction, research communication, and the design of systems people can inspect rather than simply trust.

Why I built Polinko

I built Polinko to inspect the gap between a convincing response and a grounded one.

AI systems can produce coherent answers while departing from source evidence, explicit constraints, or the limits of what they can verify. Those departures are easy to smooth over once a final answer looks acceptable.

I created Polinko to preserve those moments as research material. It gives me a way to define bounded behavioural questions, examine individual cases, keep failures visible, and revise the method when the evidence points somewhere else.

My role

I lead Polinko's research, engineering direction, and public claims.

I define the research framework, hypotheses, task boundaries, evidence criteria, exclusions, and acceptance rules. I select what enters the public record, interpret the results, and make the final publication decisions.

This matters because Polinko is an explicitly human-judged research instrument. Human judgement enters the work visibly, so authorship and responsibility should remain explicit too.

How AI participates

AI systems are collaborators, instruments, and part of what Polinko evaluates.

I use AI systems for dialogue, synthesis, reframing, bounded implementation support, validation, stress-testing, and documentation. Codex works with me inside the repository, while model APIs are part of the runtime evaluated across OCR, retrieval, response behaviour, and other bounded investigations.

The collaboration helps me develop and implement the work, but it does not transfer authorship. I select the evidence, interpret the results, authorise each claim, and make the final publication decisions.

Broader practice

My practice brings research design, interaction design, implementation, and public explanation together.

My broader practice looks at how people understand, evaluate, and collaborate with AI systems. Polinko is the current research instrument through which those questions are made operational, tested, documented, and communicated.

krystian.io gives readers the published account. The Polinko repository carries the human-led collaborative method, code, evals, decisions, and dated evidence record.
