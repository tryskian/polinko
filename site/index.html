Polinko research

Polinko evaluates AI behaviour through binary gates, evidence, and retained failures.

Polinko starts with binary eval gates because they align evaluation with binary computation: the first checkpoint is pass or fail. Signal traces and retained failures then make model behaviour inspectable without mistaking coherence for reliability.

I'm Krystian Fernando, an applied AI research engineer. I created Polinko and lead its research.

Explore the research
Why binary gates

Coherence is not the same thing as reliability.

AI systems can produce coherent output while missing a constraint, inventing a source, overstating what was checked, or adding interpretation that makes direct work harder.

Polinko keeps the first checkpoint simple: pass or fail. That binary boundary makes the evaluation legible before interpretation begins.

Reader path

Start with the research, then inspect the evidence and method.

The public site separates the question, the result, and the method so each claim stays attached to the surface that produced it.

Evidence boundary

A result stays local to the cases and conditions tested.

Passing cases do not become broad claims by default. Polinko keeps limitations, exclusions, retained failures, and unresolved questions visible alongside the result.

Authorship

The process is collaborative, and the judgement is human-owned.

I define the research questions, direct the collaborative work, contribute to the engineering, and decide what the evidence can support. AI systems are collaborators, instruments, and part of what Polinko evaluates.
