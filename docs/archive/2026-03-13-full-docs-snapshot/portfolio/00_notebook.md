## Section 2 - Applied Framework

### Human-AI Collaboration With Accountable Boundaries

This section is where you translate the theory into design/engineering principles:

- human-led framing, criteria, and judgment
- AI-accelerated iteration, implementation, refactoring
- human-owned decisions, acceptance gates, evaluation standards
- epistemic boundaries
- avoiding unearned authority
- avoiding anthropomorphic overreach
- design as behavioural governance

This is your method - the bridge between theory and engineering.

### Method Addendum: Tool/Model Separation (Authorship + Evidence Hygiene)

To keep method claims rigorous, separate assistant classes by function:

- `Codexbeab` (Nautorus build partner): implementation and validation work
  (repo changes, eval harnesses, runbook/process updates, quality-gate traces).
- `Codex notionbeab` (documentation partner): Notion IA, page structuring,
  bibliography/citation plumbing, and portfolio packaging workflows.
- `ChatGPT` and `Claude`: interaction artifacts used as research evidence objects
  (boundary/recovery transcripts, narrative-mode examples), not implementation authority.
- Human-owned layers: hypothesis design, acceptance criteria, interpretation,
  and final claims.

Reporting rule: label source channel explicitly in writeups
(`Codex build trace`, `Codex Notion curation`, `ChatGPT transcript`,
`Claude transcript`) so evidence provenance stays auditable.

### Goal: Section 2

Explain the HOW humans and AI should collaborate.

## Why This Structure Works

1. Theory -> Method -> Proof is academically rigorous.
2. It frames you as someone who understands why behaviour matters.
3. It demonstrates interdisciplinary fluency (critical theory + cognitive psych + engineering).
4. It mirrors how OpenAI structures its own internal research:
   problem -> framing -> experimental implementation.
5. It avoids ToMS entirely but keeps your strengths.
6. It showcases the collaborative nature of Nautorus - a major plus.
7. It highlights your originality without drifting into abstraction.
